Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 15, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how FlickAI ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and mobile application. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which explains how we use personal information more generally.

1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website or use an application. They allow the website or app to recognize your device and store certain information about your preferences or actions.

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies, such as web beacons (small graphic files placed on web pages or emails that allow us to monitor visits), pixels, and local storage technologies to collect information about how you use our services.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies and similar technologies:

Type Description Purpose
Essential Cookies These cookies are necessary for our website and application to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, authentication, and remembering user preferences.
Analytics Cookies These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website and application. They help us understand which pages are the most popular, how users navigate through our site, and if users encounter any errors.
Functional Cookies These cookies enable enhanced functionality. They remember your preferences and choices to provide an enhanced, personalized experience.
Marketing Cookies These cookies track your activity across websites. They are used to deliver relevant advertisements and evaluate the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

3. Specific Cookies We Use

Below is a list of the primary cookies and similar technologies we use:

3.1 First-Party Cookies (Set by FlickAI)

  • session_id - Essential cookie for managing user sessions
  • user_preferences - Functional cookie to remember your settings
  • auth_token - Essential cookie for authentication
  • app_theme - Functional cookie to remember your preferred display theme (light/dark)
  • app_language - Functional cookie to remember your language preference

3.2 Third-Party Cookies

  • Google Analytics - Analytics cookies to collect information about how users interact with our website and app
  • Firebase Analytics - Analytics cookies for app usage statistics
  • Azure Application Insights - Analytics and performance monitoring for our web services

3.3 Mobile App Tracking Technologies

In our mobile application, we use:

  • Device Identifiers - Used to identify unique installations of our app on devices
  • Local Storage - To store user preferences and cached data
  • Mobile Analytics Tools - To analyze app usage patterns

4. Managing Cookies

4.1 Website Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences. You can:

  • Delete cookies from your device
  • Block cookies by activating the setting on your browser that allows you to refuse all or some cookies
  • Set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie

Please note that if you choose to block or delete cookies, you may not be able to access certain areas or features of our website, and some services may not function properly.

4.2 Instructions for Managing Cookies in Common Browsers

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Site permissions → Cookies and site data

4.3 Mobile App Data

For our mobile application, you can manage data collection through your device settings:

  • iOS devices: Settings → Privacy → Advertising → Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android devices: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of interest-based ads

You can also manage local storage data by clearing app data through your device settings or uninstalling the app.

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that allows you to tell websites that you do not want to have your online activities tracked. Currently, our systems do not recognize browser "Do Not Track" requests.

6. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by an updated "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about our use of cookies.
